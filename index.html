The Bitcoin Standard, Fact or Fiction?

Bitcoin has taken the world by storm. Whether it will stand the test of time, as a store of value, or not, is still up for debate.

Proof-Of-Work

Proof-Of-Work is the consensus mechanism which secures the Bitcoin network.

Neutrality

Anyone with the necessary hardware can become a miner. There is no central authority.

Fixed Supply

Bitcoin has a predictable inflation rate, and has a fixed supply. This makes it a great unit of account.

Is Bitcoin Sound Money?

Throughout history we have used many forms of money to act as a medium of exchange between people. We transitioned to money, from the barter system, which became increasingly untenable as civilisation expanded.

Humans used precious metals like Gold and Silver for thousands of years, however, a few decades ago we transitioned to fiat which isn't backed by anything. This has led to unconstrained inflation, reducing the purchasing power that money has.

There are three main properties that make good money. It has to be a good store of value, medium of exchange and unit of account.

As a store of value, Bitcoin seems to fare quite well, considering how much the value has increased since its inception. It's also incredibly value dense, considering it can absorb an indefinite amount of value.

As a medium of exchange, it works very well. All you need is a private key to access your funds, without the permission from anyone. Bitcoin is also divisible into tens of millions of pieces. And for all practical purposes, it is fungible.

Finally, as a unit of account, it seems to be far better than the currencies that we use today. This is due to the predictable and fixed supply. It isn't inflated in an unconstrained manner, as is the case with fiat currencies.
